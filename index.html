Real-time Machine Learning Competition on Data Streams

A Track in the IEEE Big Data 2019 Big Data Cup

Description of the Tasks

  • This track in the IEEE Big Data 2019 Big Data Cup represents the first of its kind, Machine Learning competition on data streams. The main idea is to build incremental classifiers and provide the predictions for the data instances that are arriving in stream.

  • Data streams, such as data originated from sensors, have increasingly gained the interest of researchers and companies and are currently widely studied in data science. For example, companies in the telecommunication and energy industry are trying to exploit these data and get real-time insights on their services and equipment. To be able to extract valuable knowledge from data streams, one must be able to analyze the data as they arrive and make meaningful predictions. For this purpose, we use fast incremental learners. For batch learners there already exists a community that is organizing various competitions on machine learning tasks to obtain models with better performance. We want to introduce the same approach to engage the whole community in solving essential problems in data stream mining. We propose a new kind of data science competition based on a real-time prediction setting, using our novel platform on data streams. The examples to predict are going to be sent in real-time, and the predictions should also be submitted in real-time. To the best of our knowledge, this will be the first data science competition conducted in real-time. The task of the competition is to predict network activity, and the data will be provided from one of our partner companies.
